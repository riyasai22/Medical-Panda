Description

Parkinson's disease is caused by a loss of nerve cells in the part of the brain called the substantia nigra. Nerve cells in this part of the brain are responsible for producing a chemical called dopamine.The biggest risk factor for developing Parkinson's is advancing age. The average age of onset is 60. Men are more likely to develop Parkinson's disease than women.

Signs & Symptoms

Tremor

Slowed movement

Rigid muscles

Impaired posture and balance

Speech changes

Loss of automatic movements

Writing changes
